Official Game Guide & Manual

Introduction

Side Scroller Sprint is a high-intensity, endless runner arcade game developed in HTML5. Designed to test reaction time and precision, the game strips away complex mechanics to focus on one core skill: timing. As the player accelerates through a neon-styled digital landscape, obstacles appear with increasing frequency and speed. Your objective is simple but challenging—survive as long as possible to climb the global leaderboard.

How to Play

The controls are optimized for both desktop and mobile devices, ensuring a seamless experience across platforms:

  • Desktop: Press the SPACEBAR or the UP ARROW key to perform a jump.
  • Mobile / Tablet: Tap anywhere on the screen (or the dedicated touch layer) to jump.
  • The Goal: Clear the incoming colored crates and obstacles. Hitting any part of an obstacle ends the run immediately.

Game Mechanics & Scoring

Unlike standard platformers, Side Scroller Sprint utilizes a progressive velocity system. Every 500 frames, the game speed increases incrementally. This means the rhythm you establish in the first 30 seconds will not work one minute in.

Scoring: You earn 1 point for every obstacle successfully cleared.
Global Leaderboard: This game is integrated with a real-time Firebase database. If you beat your personal high score, it is automatically compared against players worldwide. Only the top 5 scores make it to the main menu display.

Strategy & Tips

To reach the top of the leaderboard, players must master the "short hop" vs. "long jump" timing. While the jump height is fixed, initiating a jump too early will cause you to land on the obstacle, triggering a game over. Initiating too late leads to a collision.

Pro Tip: Focus your eyes on the right side of the screen rather than your character. Reacting to obstacles as they spawn gives you a split-second advantage compared to watching them arrive at the player's position.

Technical Specs: Built with Vanilla JavaScript and HTML5 Canvas. Powered by Google Firebase for real-time data persistence.
